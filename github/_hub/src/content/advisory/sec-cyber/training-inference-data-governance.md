The data used to train, fine-tune, and serve AI models represents a cybersecurity risk that should be incorporated into the risk management strategy disclosed under Item 106(b). Training data may contain personally identifiable information, proprietary business logic, or trade secrets — categories of data whose unauthorized access or disclosure could be material. Governance controls over this data demonstrate the kind of proactive risk management the SEC expects companies to describe.

Companies should evaluate whether their AI data governance practices are mature enough to support accurate disclosure. This includes data classification for training sets, access controls on inference pipelines, monitoring for data poisoning or extraction attacks, and incident response procedures specific to AI data compromise scenarios.
