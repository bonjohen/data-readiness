When AI agents operate in environments where their actions could trigger cybersecurity incidents or affect material business processes, human review gates become a governance control relevant to SEC disclosure. Item 106(c) requires describing board oversight of cybersecurity risk — if AI systems are making decisions that could have material impact, the board's oversight of those systems is a legitimate disclosure topic.

Human review gates demonstrate governance maturity by showing that the organization has identified where autonomous AI decisions intersect with material risk and has implemented controls proportional to that risk. This is particularly relevant for AI systems involved in security operations, access management, or financial data processing.
