AI models trained on financial data — whether for risk scoring, fraud detection, regulatory reporting, or customer analytics — must operate within DORA's ICT risk management framework. Training data governance includes ensuring data integrity, documenting data lineage from source through transformation to model input, and maintaining quality controls that prevent data corruption or poisoning from undermining model reliability.

Model validation for regulatory compliance is particularly important in financial services where model outputs may affect capital calculations, credit decisions, or regulatory reports. Financial entities should establish validation procedures that test model accuracy and robustness under stressed conditions, document model limitations, and maintain version control that supports regulatory examination. The data governance framework should enable the entity to demonstrate to competent authorities exactly what data was used, how it was processed, and what controls ensured its integrity.
