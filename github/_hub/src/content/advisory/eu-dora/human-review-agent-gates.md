DORA's governance framework places ultimate responsibility on the management body for ICT risk, which includes the risk introduced by autonomous AI agents operating within financial processes. Where AI systems make or influence decisions affecting trading, lending, compliance screening, or customer-facing services, human review gates must be established at defined decision thresholds to satisfy the oversight obligation.

Escalation procedures should address scenarios where AI agents produce anomalous outputs, encounter edge cases outside their training distribution, or interact with systems supporting critical or important functions. These escalation paths connect to the broader DORA incident management framework — an AI system malfunction that disrupts critical services may constitute an ICT-related incident requiring classification and potential reporting to competent authorities.
