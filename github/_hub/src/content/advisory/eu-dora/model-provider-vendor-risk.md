AI model providers — whether offering foundation models via API, fine-tuning platforms, or embedded AI services — fall squarely within DORA's definition of ICT third-party service providers. Financial entities must include these providers in their register of information on ICT third-party arrangements, assess concentration risk arising from sector-wide dependency on a small number of model providers, and ensure contracts contain DORA-mandated provisions including audit rights, data location requirements, and exit strategies.

Where AI models support critical or important functions, the financial entity's exit strategy must address model portability and continuity of service upon provider termination. If a model provider is designated as a critical ICT third-party service provider by the ESAs, it becomes subject to the EU-level oversight framework — a possibility that financial entities should factor into their vendor risk planning and contractual negotiations.
