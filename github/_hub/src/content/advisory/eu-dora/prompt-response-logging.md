DORA's incident detection requirements demand that financial entities maintain monitoring capabilities across their ICT systems — and AI systems are no exception. Logging prompts and responses to AI models used in financial operations creates the observability needed to detect anomalous behavior, support incident investigation, and provide competent authorities with evidence during regulatory examinations.

The logging architecture must balance thoroughness with data protection requirements. Financial entities should classify logged AI interactions according to their ICT asset criticality framework, applying appropriate retention periods and access controls. When AI interactions involve personal data or commercially sensitive information, the logging design must account for GDPR obligations alongside DORA's operational resilience requirements.
