    <h2>Why the SHIELD Act Matters</h2>
    <p>The NY SHIELD Act applies to any business that holds private information of New York residents, regardless of where the business is located. Without reasonable safeguards, your organization faces AG enforcement, breach notification obligations, and market access risk.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Access the New York Market</h3>
        <p>The SHIELD Act applies to any business holding NY residents' private information, regardless of where the business is located. Operating in the New York market requires compliance.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet Reasonable Safeguards Standard</h3>
        <p>The Act requires "reasonable" administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the size and complexity of your business and the nature of the data you hold.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Prepare for Breach Notification</h3>
        <p>Expanded breach definitions and notification requirements affect incident response. The SHIELD Act broadened what counts as a breach and who must be notified.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Demonstrate Due Diligence</h3>
        <p>Reasonable safeguards compliance demonstrates due diligence in the event of a breach investigation by the New York Attorney General.</p>
      </div>
