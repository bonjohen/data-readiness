  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why CCPA / CPRA Matters</h2>
    <p>California's privacy laws set the standard for consumer data protection in the United States. Companies that handle California residents' personal information face direct regulatory scrutiny, litigation exposure, and competitive pressure to demonstrate privacy maturity.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet California Privacy Requirements</h3>
        <p>CCPA/CPRA are the strictest state privacy laws in the United States. Compliance is mandatory for businesses meeting revenue, data-volume, or data-sale thresholds that collect California residents' personal information.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Build Consumer Trust</h3>
        <p>Demonstrate commitment to data privacy through transparent practices, responsive consumer rights fulfillment, and clear privacy notices that build long-term customer relationships.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Reduce Litigation Risk</h3>
        <p>CCPA's private right of action for data breaches creates direct financial liability. Proactive compliance with security and privacy requirements reduces exposure to costly lawsuits.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Enterprise Privacy Maturity</h3>
        <p>A strong CCPA/CPRA posture signals privacy maturity to enterprise partners, investors, and customers &mdash; differentiating your organization in competitive markets.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
