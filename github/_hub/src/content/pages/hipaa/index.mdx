  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why HIPAA Security Rule Matters</h2>
    <p>Healthcare organizations and their business associates must demonstrate HIPAA Security Rule compliance to protect electronic protected health information. Without it, you risk enforcement actions, lost partnerships, and compromised patient data.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Access the Healthcare Market</h3>
        <p>Covered entities require business associates to demonstrate HIPAA compliance before sharing ePHI. Without documented safeguards, healthcare partnerships are off the table.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Protect Against OCR Enforcement</h3>
        <p>OCR enforces HIPAA with penalties up to $2.1M per violation category per year. Proactive readiness reduces enforcement risk and demonstrates good faith compliance efforts.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Safeguard ePHI</h3>
        <p>The Security Rule requires administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of electronic protected health information.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Enable Business Associate Relationships</h3>
        <p>BAAs are contractually required &mdash; compliance opens partnership doors with hospitals, health plans, and other covered entities that need technology partners they can trust.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
