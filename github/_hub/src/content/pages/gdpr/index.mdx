  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why GDPR Matters</h2>
    <p>The General Data Protection Regulation is the world's most comprehensive data protection framework. For technology companies serving EU customers or processing EU residents' data, GDPR compliance is not optional &mdash; it's the price of market access.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Access the EU Market</h3>
        <p>GDPR compliance is non-negotiable for serving EU customers. Without it, enterprise buyers, partners, and distributors in the EU and EEA will not engage.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Avoid Significant Penalties</h3>
        <p>Fines up to &euro;20 million or 4% of global annual turnover &mdash; whichever is higher &mdash; make GDPR enforcement among the most consequential in any regulatory regime.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet Processor Obligations</h3>
        <p>If you process data on behalf of EU controllers, GDPR compliance is contractually required. Controllers will audit your practices and demand documented accountability.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Enable Cross-Border Operations</h3>
        <p>Proper transfer mechanisms &mdash; SCCs, adequacy decisions, binding corporate rules &mdash; enable global data flows without legal exposure.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
