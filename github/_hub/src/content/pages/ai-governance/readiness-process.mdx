<div class="section">
  <h2>Phase Details</h2>

  <div class="phase-detail">
    <h3>1. Intake &amp; Scoping <span class="phase-duration">Week 1</span></h3>
    <p>We start by understanding your AI landscape and current governance posture.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>AI system inventory</strong> &mdash; catalog all AI systems in development and production, including purpose, data sources, and deployment context</li>
      <li><strong>Current governance practices review</strong> &mdash; assess existing policies, processes, and oversight mechanisms for AI development</li>
      <li><strong>Regulatory applicability analysis</strong> &mdash; determine which frameworks apply (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, state laws, sector-specific requirements)</li>
      <li><strong>Stakeholder mapping</strong> &mdash; identify AI developers, deployers, risk owners, and affected populations</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="phase-detail">
    <h3>2. Assessment <span class="phase-duration">Week 2&ndash;3</span></h3>
    <p>We evaluate your AI systems and practices against governance framework requirements.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Risk categorization of AI systems</strong> &mdash; classify each system by risk level using applicable framework criteria</li>
      <li><strong>Bias and fairness evaluation</strong> &mdash; assess current testing practices and identify gaps in bias detection and mitigation</li>
      <li><strong>Transparency and explainability assessment</strong> &mdash; evaluate whether AI decisions are documented and explainable to affected individuals</li>
      <li><strong>Human oversight mechanism review</strong> &mdash; assess whether appropriate human review and intervention mechanisms exist</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="phase-detail">
    <h3>3. Outputs <span class="phase-duration">Week 3&ndash;4</span></h3>
    <p>We deliver the artifacts that establish your AI governance framework.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>AI governance framework</strong> &mdash; policies, roles, processes, and oversight structure tailored to your organization</li>
      <li><strong>Risk categorization matrix</strong> &mdash; every AI system classified with risk level, rationale, and required controls</li>
      <li><strong>Bias testing methodology</strong> &mdash; defined approach to testing for bias and fairness in high-risk AI systems</li>
      <li><strong>Transparency documentation templates</strong> &mdash; standardized formats for documenting AI decisions, data sources, and model behavior</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="phase-detail">
    <h3>4. Follow-on <span class="phase-duration">Ongoing</span></h3>
    <p>After the readiness sprint, ongoing activities keep your governance program current.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Model monitoring program</strong> &mdash; continuous monitoring of AI system performance, drift, and fairness metrics</li>
      <li><strong>Regulatory change tracking</strong> &mdash; monitor evolving AI regulations and update governance practices accordingly</li>
      <li><strong>Annual governance review</strong> &mdash; periodic assessment of governance framework effectiveness and completeness</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
