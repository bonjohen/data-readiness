  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why COPPA Matters</h2>
    <p>Children's online privacy is under increasing regulatory scrutiny. Companies whose products or services may be used by children under 13 face strict FTC enforcement, reputational risk, and the responsibility to earn the trust of parents and educators.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Protect Children's Privacy</h3>
        <p>COPPA is the primary federal law governing children's online privacy. It requires verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information from children under 13.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Avoid FTC Enforcement</h3>
        <p>The FTC actively enforces COPPA with penalties reaching millions of dollars. Non-compliance creates significant financial and reputational exposure.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Enable Safe Harbor Participation</h3>
        <p>COPPA safe harbor programs provide streamlined compliance paths and industry-specific guidance, reducing regulatory uncertainty for qualifying companies.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Build Parental Trust</h3>
        <p>Demonstrating COPPA compliance builds confidence with parents and schools, opening doors to the education technology and children's content markets.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
