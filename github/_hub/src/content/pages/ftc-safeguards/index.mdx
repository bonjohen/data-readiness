    <h2>Why the Safeguards Rule Matters</h2>
    <p>The FTC's amended Safeguards Rule imposes comprehensive information security requirements on financial institutions. The 2021 amendments (effective June 2023) introduced specific technical controls, qualified individual requirements, and board reporting obligations that demand a structured security program.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet Regulatory Requirements</h3>
        <p>The Safeguards Rule applies to financial institutions under FTC jurisdiction, including mortgage brokers, motor vehicle dealers, payday lenders, tax preparers, and other non-banking financial entities.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Protect Customer Information</h3>
        <p>The amended rule requires comprehensive security programs with specific technical controls including encryption, MFA, access controls, and incident response capabilities.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Avoid FTC Enforcement</h3>
        <p>The FTC actively enforces the Safeguards Rule with consent orders and penalties. Non-compliance creates significant financial and operational exposure for covered institutions.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Demonstrate Security Maturity</h3>
        <p>A Safeguards-compliant program demonstrates security maturity to partners, customers, and regulators &mdash; differentiating your institution in a competitive market.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
