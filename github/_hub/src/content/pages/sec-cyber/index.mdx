  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Matters</h2>
    <p>Public companies must disclose material cybersecurity incidents and describe their cyber risk governance. Without robust disclosure processes, companies face enforcement risk, investor concern, and reputational damage.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet SEC Disclosure Requirements</h3>
        <p>Public companies must disclose material cybersecurity incidents on Form 8-K within 4 business days of determining materiality &mdash; a tight timeline that demands established processes.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Demonstrate Board Governance</h3>
        <p>Annual 10-K disclosures must describe board oversight of cybersecurity risk, including how the board is informed about and monitors cyber threats.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Protect Investor Confidence</h3>
        <p>Transparent cyber risk disclosure builds investor trust and reduces litigation risk. Investors expect companies to manage and disclose cyber risk proactively.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Establish Materiality Processes</h3>
        <p>Having a defined materiality determination process is critical for timely and accurate disclosure &mdash; without it, companies risk late filings or inadequate disclosures.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </section>
