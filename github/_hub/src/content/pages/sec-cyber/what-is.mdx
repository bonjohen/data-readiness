    <h1>What is SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure?</h1>

    <p>SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules (effective December 2023, adopted July 2023) require public companies to disclose material cybersecurity incidents on Form 8-K (Item 1.05) and describe cybersecurity risk management, strategy, and governance in annual 10-K reports (Items 106(b) and 106(c)).</p>

    <p>The rules apply to all SEC registrants &mdash; public companies filing with the SEC. They represent the most significant federal cybersecurity disclosure mandate for public companies, creating enforceable requirements for both incident reporting and ongoing governance transparency.</p>

    <div class="section">
      <h2>Key Requirements</h2>
      <p>The SEC cybersecurity rules create two primary disclosure obligations with distinct timelines and scopes:</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Material Incident Disclosure (8-K)</strong> &mdash; disclose material cybersecurity incidents within 4 business days of materiality determination, describing the nature, scope, timing, and material impact</li>
        <li><strong>Materiality Determination Process</strong> &mdash; establish and document a defined process for determining whether a cybersecurity incident is material to investors</li>
        <li><strong>Annual Risk Management Disclosure (10-K)</strong> &mdash; describe cybersecurity risk management processes, including how risks are identified, assessed, and managed</li>
        <li><strong>Board Oversight Disclosure</strong> &mdash; describe the board's role in overseeing cybersecurity risk, including how and how frequently the board is informed</li>
        <li><strong>Management Role Disclosure</strong> &mdash; describe management's role in assessing and managing cybersecurity risk, including relevant expertise</li>
        <li><strong>Third-Party Risk Disclosure</strong> &mdash; describe how cybersecurity risks from third-party service providers are assessed and managed</li>
        <li><strong>Incident Response Integration</strong> &mdash; ensure incident response processes feed into the materiality determination and disclosure workflow</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
      <h2>Materiality Determination Framework</h2>
      <p>Materiality is the linchpin of incident disclosure. The SEC uses the traditional securities-law standard: information is material if a reasonable investor would consider it important in making an investment decision.</p>

      <div class="table-wrapper">
        <table>
          <thead>
            <tr>
              <th>Factor</th>
              <th>Considerations</th>
            </tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Financial Impact</strong></td>
              <td>Direct costs, remediation expenses, lost revenue, regulatory fines</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Operational Impact</strong></td>
              <td>Business disruption, system downtime, data loss, service degradation</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Reputational Impact</strong></td>
              <td>Customer trust, brand damage, market perception, competitive position</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Legal/Regulatory Impact</strong></td>
              <td>Litigation risk, regulatory actions, contractual obligations, insurance</td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td><strong>Data Sensitivity</strong></td>
              <td>Type and volume of data compromised, affected individuals, notification obligations</td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="section">
      <h2>Readiness Assessment Checklist</h2>
      <p>Before your next filing cycle, evaluate where your organization stands against these readiness questions:</p>

      <ol class="checklist">
        <li>Is there a defined materiality determination process with clear roles, criteria, and escalation paths?</li>
        <li>Are 8-K filing procedures documented, including templates, review workflows, and the 4-business-day timeline?</li>
        <li>Has the 10-K cybersecurity disclosure been drafted or updated to describe risk management, strategy, and governance?</li>
        <li>Are board oversight mechanisms for cybersecurity risk documented, including frequency and format of briefings?</li>
        <li>Are management roles and cybersecurity expertise described in disclosure-ready language?</li>
        <li>Is third-party cybersecurity risk management described and integrated into the disclosure framework?</li>
      </ol>

      <p>If you can't confidently answer "yes" to most of these, a <a href="readiness-process">readiness sprint</a> will get you there.</p>
