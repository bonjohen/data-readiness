  <section class="content section">
    <h2>Why PCI DSS Matters</h2>
    <p>Any organization that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data must comply with PCI DSS. Without it, your merchant agreement is at risk, breach liability increases, and payment processing privileges can be revoked.</p>
    <div class="value-grid">
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Maintain Payment Processing</h3>
        <p>PCI DSS compliance is required to accept, process, or transmit cardholder data. Without validated compliance, acquiring banks can revoke your merchant processing privileges.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Meet Acquiring Bank Requirements</h3>
        <p>Acquiring banks mandate PCI DSS compliance as a condition of merchant agreements. Non-compliance puts your ability to process payments at risk.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Protect Against Data Breaches</h3>
        <p>PCI DSS controls directly reduce the risk of payment data compromise. Non-compliance at the time of a breach carries severe financial and operational consequences.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="value-item">
        <h3>Demonstrate Security to Partners</h3>
        <p>PCI DSS compliance signals mature security practices to payment partners and customers, building trust and enabling business relationships in the payment ecosystem.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
