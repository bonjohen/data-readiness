The NY SHIELD Act explicitly identifies employee training as a component of the administrative safeguards that constitute a reasonable security program. Section 899-bb(2)(b)(i)(C) requires organizations to "train and manage employees in security program practices and procedures," recognizing that even the most sophisticated technical controls can be defeated by employees who do not understand their role in protecting private information. Training programs must be substantive and role-appropriate — a database administrator needs different security training than a customer service representative, though both need foundational awareness of the organization's security policies.

Effective training programs address the full spectrum of threats that employees may encounter. This includes recognizing phishing emails and social engineering attempts, understanding proper handling procedures for private information, knowing when and how to report suspected security incidents, and following clean desk and secure workstation practices. Training should incorporate real-world examples and practical exercises, such as phishing simulations, rather than relying solely on passive presentation formats. Comprehension verification — through quizzes, interactive assessments, or practical demonstrations — ensures that employees are not merely completing training but actually absorbing the material.

The "ongoing" dimension of the training requirement reflects the reality that threats evolve continuously. A training program delivered once during onboarding and never revisited fails to address new attack techniques, changes to organizational policies, or shifts in the regulatory landscape. Organizations should establish a regular training cadence — annual comprehensive training supplemented by periodic updates on emerging threats — and should track completion and comprehension metrics to identify employees or departments that may need additional attention. Training records serve as critical evidence that the organization takes the training mandate seriously and has invested in building a security-aware workforce.
