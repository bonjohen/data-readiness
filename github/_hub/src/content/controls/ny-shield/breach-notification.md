Section 899-aa establishes New York's breach notification obligations, which were significantly expanded by the SHIELD Act in 2019. The law requires any person or business that owns or licenses computerized data containing private information of a New York resident to disclose a breach to affected individuals in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay. The statute defines a breach broadly: unauthorized access to or acquisition of computerized data that compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of private information. Notably, the SHIELD Act expanded the trigger beyond acquisition to include unauthorized access, lowering the threshold from the original 2005 notification law.

Notification to state agencies is a critical compliance obligation. The entity must notify the Attorney General, the Department of Financial Services, and the Division of State Police, providing them with the timing, content, and distribution of the notice to affected residents. When more than 5,000 New York residents must be notified, the entity must also notify consumer reporting agencies of the timing, content, and distribution of notices. These agency notifications serve both enforcement and consumer protection purposes, enabling regulators to monitor breach trends and coordinate response efforts.

The content and method requirements ensure that affected individuals receive actionable information. Notifications must describe the breach, identify the categories of private information involved, provide contact information for the notifying entity, and include contact details for the Attorney General and FTC. The statute permits written, electronic, or telephone notice, with substitute notice (email, conspicuous website posting, and major statewide media notification) available when direct notification would cost more than $250,000, affect more than 500,000 persons, or when the entity lacks sufficient contact information. Organizations should maintain pre-drafted notification templates and establish clear internal escalation procedures to meet the expedient notification standard.
