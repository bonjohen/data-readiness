The FTC Safeguards Rule requires every financial institution to designate a single qualified individual who is responsible for overseeing, implementing, and enforcing its information security program. This individual does not need to be an employee — the role can be outsourced to a third-party service provider — but the institution retains ultimate accountability. The qualified individual must have sufficient authority to direct security investments, mandate policy changes, and enforce compliance across all business units that handle customer information.

A critical obligation of the qualified individual is the annual written report to the board of directors or equivalent governing body. This report must cover the overall status of the information security program, material events including security incidents and their response, and the results of the most recent risk assessment. The report serves as both a governance artifact and a mechanism for ensuring that senior leadership remains informed about the institution's security posture and any gaps that require investment or attention.

Documentation of the qualified individual's qualifications is not optional. The rule expects that the designated person possesses the knowledge, skills, and experience necessary to manage the security program effectively. This includes relevant certifications, ongoing training in emerging threats and regulatory changes, and demonstrated experience with information security program management in a financial services context. When the role is outsourced, the institution must document the basis for selecting the provider and maintain oversight of their performance.
