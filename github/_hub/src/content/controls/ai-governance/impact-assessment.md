The MAP function of the AI RMF requires organizations to understand the context in which their AI systems operate and the impacts those systems have on all stakeholders. Impact assessment goes beyond technical performance measurement to examine who is affected, how they are affected, and whether the deployment context introduces risks that the system's design did not anticipate.

A rigorous impact assessment examines harms across multiple dimensions: individual harms (incorrect decisions, privacy violations, loss of autonomy), group harms (discriminatory outcomes against protected classes), societal harms (erosion of trust, labor displacement), and environmental harms (compute resource consumption, e-waste). Each dimension requires different assessment methods — individual harms may be measurable through testing, while societal harms require qualitative stakeholder engagement.

The comparative analysis requirement is particularly important and frequently overlooked. Organizations should document why an AI approach was chosen over non-AI alternatives and what specific benefits the AI system provides that justify its risks. This analysis prevents the adoption of AI for its own sake and ensures that the added complexity and opacity of AI systems delivers genuine value to the organization and its stakeholders.
