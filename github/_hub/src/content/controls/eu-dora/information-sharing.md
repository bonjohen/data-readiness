DORA Chapter VI (Article 45) encourages — though does not mandate — financial entities to participate in cyber threat intelligence sharing arrangements. While voluntary, this provision reflects the recognition that individual entities benefit from collective intelligence about threats targeting the financial sector. Participation in sector-specific information sharing and analysis centres (ISACs) and similar trusted communities is explicitly encouraged.

Financial entities that participate must establish agreements governing what information is shared, with whom, and under what protections. Shared information must be handled with appropriate confidentiality safeguards, data protection compliance (particularly regarding personal data), and competition law awareness. The regulation specifically addresses the tension between sharing enough to be useful and protecting commercially sensitive information.

Competent authorities must be notified when a financial entity joins or leaves an information sharing arrangement. This notification requirement ensures supervisors maintain visibility into the threat intelligence ecosystem across their jurisdiction. For organizations considering participation, the operational benefits — early warning of sector-specific threats, shared indicators of compromise, and access to peer-validated detection rules — typically outweigh the administrative overhead of establishing and maintaining the necessary agreements and handling procedures.
