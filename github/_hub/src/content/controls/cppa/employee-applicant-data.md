The original CCPA included temporary exemptions for employee and B2B personal information, but CPRA allowed those exemptions to sunset on January 1, 2023. As a result, employees, job applicants, independent contractors, and B2B contacts now have the full suite of consumer rights: the right to know what PI is collected about them, the right to delete, the right to correct inaccuracies, and the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of their PI. This extension significantly expands the operational scope of CCPA/CPRA compliance because HR systems, applicant tracking systems, payroll processors, and benefits administrators all process substantial volumes of employee PI, much of it sensitive.

The workforce privacy notice must be provided at or before the point of collection and must identify the categories of PI collected and the purposes for which they are used. For employees, this covers everything from payroll and tax information to badge access logs, IT monitoring data, background check results, and benefits enrollment data. Many of these categories qualify as sensitive personal information under §1798.140(ae), triggering the additional SPI obligations. Organizations should inventory their HR data flows with the same rigor applied to consumer-facing systems.

Common gaps include: treating the employee privacy notice as a one-time onboarding document rather than a living disclosure that must be updated when practices change, failing to account for PI processed by HR technology vendors (applicant tracking systems, learning management systems, performance review platforms) in the vendor agreement framework, and lacking a process for handling employee data subject requests. HR data retention is particularly complex because employment law imposes its own retention requirements (EEOC records, I-9 forms, tax records) that may conflict with CPRA minimization principles. Retention schedules should document both the CPRA basis and the employment law basis for each category, applying the longer of the two where they conflict.
