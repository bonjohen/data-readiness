Regulation S-K Item 106 (17 CFR § 229.106) requires annual disclosure in the 10-K covering three areas: cybersecurity risk management and strategy (Item 106(b)), governance by the board of directors (Item 106(c)(1)), and governance by management (Item 106(c)(2)). Unlike the 8-K incident disclosure, this is a standing annual obligation regardless of whether any incidents occurred during the period. The disclosure is narrative — the SEC expects a substantive description of your actual practices, not boilerplate.

Drafting the 10-K cybersecurity sections requires coordination across the CISO, legal, finance, and the board secretary. The risk management section should describe how the company identifies and assesses cyber risks, what tools and frameworks it uses, how it integrates cyber risk into the enterprise risk management program, and whether it engages third-party assessors. The governance section should identify the board committee responsible for cyber risk, explain the frequency and substance of board briefings, and describe management's relevant expertise including certifications, prior experience, and reporting lines.

Companies should begin drafting well before the filing deadline, circulating drafts through legal review and cross-functional sign-off. Year-over-year consistency matters — the SEC and investors will compare current disclosures to prior filings, so material changes in risk management approach, governance structure, or management roles should be called out explicitly.
