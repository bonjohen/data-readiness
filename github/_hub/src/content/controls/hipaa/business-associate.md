Business associate agreements under 45 CFR § 164.308(b) are a critical mechanism for extending HIPAA Security Rule protections to third parties that create, receive, maintain, or transmit ePHI on behalf of a covered entity. The 2013 Omnibus Rule made business associates directly liable for Security Rule compliance, but the contractual framework of the BAA remains the primary enforcement tool for covered entities to ensure their vendors meet obligations.

A compliant BAA must specify the permitted and required uses and disclosures of ePHI, require the business associate to implement appropriate safeguards, mandate reporting of security incidents, and require that subcontractors agree to the same restrictions. Generic or template agreements that lack specificity about the actual ePHI involved and the safeguards expected are a common deficiency found during OCR investigations.

Organizations should maintain a comprehensive BAA register that tracks all business associate relationships, agreement status, renewal dates, and risk assessments. Regular due diligence — reviewing a BA's security posture, incident history, and subcontractor relationships — demonstrates ongoing vigilance. OCR has levied significant penalties against covered entities that failed to execute BAAs or that relied on outdated agreements that predated the Omnibus Rule requirements.
