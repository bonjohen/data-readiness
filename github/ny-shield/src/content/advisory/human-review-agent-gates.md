The SHIELD Act's administrative safeguards require organizations to manage personnel in security practices — a principle that extends naturally to AI agents operating within business processes that touch private information. When an AI agent can access, process, or make decisions about data that includes Social Security numbers, biometric identifiers, or email-password combinations, the organization must ensure that the agent operates under controls equivalent to those applied to human employees. Without human review gates, AI agents can make decisions about private information without the oversight that the statute's administrative safeguard framework contemplates.

Organizations should implement approval checkpoints at critical decision points where AI agents interact with private information, maintain comprehensive audit logs of agent actions for breach investigation and notification purposes, and establish clear escalation paths for situations where an agent encounters data that may fall within the SHIELD Act's expanded private information definition. These controls support the organization's ability to demonstrate that its security program addresses risks introduced by automated systems, not just human actors.
