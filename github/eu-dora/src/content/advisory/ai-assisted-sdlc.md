Financial entities adopting AI-assisted development must integrate these tools into their existing DORA change management and ICT security frameworks. AI-generated code changes require the same risk assessment, testing, and approval processes as human-written code — and potentially heightened scrutiny given the opacity of AI suggestions and their potential to introduce vulnerabilities into systems supporting critical financial functions.

AI coding assistants themselves may constitute ICT third-party services under DORA Chapter V, requiring inclusion in the entity's ICT third-party register and risk assessment. Organizations should evaluate concentration risk if development teams depend heavily on a single AI coding platform, and ensure contractual arrangements address data handling, service continuity, and the provider's own security posture.
