DORA Chapter II (Articles 5–16) establishes the ICT risk management framework as the foundational obligation for financial entities. The framework must be comprehensive, documented, and approved by the management body, covering the full lifecycle from asset identification through continuous improvement. Financial entities must identify all ICT-supported business functions and map dependencies to ensure that risks to digital operational resilience are understood and managed proportionately.

The framework requires layered controls: protection and prevention measures (security policies, access controls, encryption, network security), detection capabilities for anomalous activities, and documented response and recovery procedures. These are not standalone activities but interconnected elements that the management body must oversee as an integrated system. Critical ICT third-party service providers supporting these functions must also be accounted for within the framework's scope.

Continuous learning is a distinguishing feature of DORA's approach. Article 13 requires financial entities to incorporate lessons from ICT-related incidents, resilience testing results, and threat intelligence into the framework. This creates a feedback loop where the framework evolves based on operational experience, regulatory guidance (including RTS and ITS delegated acts), and the changing threat landscape — moving beyond static compliance toward genuine operational resilience.
