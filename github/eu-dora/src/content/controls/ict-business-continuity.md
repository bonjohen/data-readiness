Articles 11 and 12 of DORA establish detailed requirements for ICT business continuity management that go beyond traditional disaster recovery. Financial entities must develop a comprehensive ICT business continuity policy that integrates with the overall business continuity framework and specifically addresses the digital operational resilience dimension. This includes documented response and recovery plans with defined recovery time objectives (RTOs) and recovery point objectives (RPOs) for critical and important functions.

Crisis communication is treated as a distinct obligation. Financial entities must have plans for communicating with internal staff, external clients, counterparties, and competent authorities during ICT disruptions. These plans must address different severity levels and include pre-approved message templates, designated spokespersons, and escalation procedures — recognizing that communication failures during a crisis can amplify operational and reputational damage.

Annual testing is mandatory and must include scenario-based exercises that stress-test the response and recovery plans under realistic conditions. The testing programme should cover switchover to backup sites, failover of critical systems, and activation of crisis communication procedures. Results must be documented, with identified gaps driving plan updates. When critical ICT third-party service providers are involved, they should participate in testing exercises to validate that end-to-end recovery works across organizational boundaries.
