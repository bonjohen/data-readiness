DORA Chapter V (Articles 28–44) represents one of the regulation's most significant innovations: a comprehensive framework for managing ICT third-party risk across the financial sector. Financial entities must adopt a strategy on ICT third-party risk, maintain a register of all ICT third-party arrangements, and ensure that contracts with ICT service providers contain specific provisions mandated by the regulation — including service level descriptions, data location requirements, audit and access rights, exit clauses, and sub-outsourcing restrictions.

The register of information is particularly consequential. Financial entities must maintain a complete, up-to-date register of all contractual arrangements with ICT third-party service providers, including intra-group arrangements. This register must be reportable to competent authorities and forms the basis for concentration risk assessment — identifying where the financial entity (or the sector as a whole) depends too heavily on a single provider or a small number of providers for critical functions.

DORA also introduces a novel EU-level oversight framework for critical ICT third-party service providers designated by the European Supervisory Authorities. These providers — typically major cloud platforms and core banking system vendors — become subject to direct oversight by a Lead Overseer, with powers to conduct inspections, issue recommendations, and ultimately impose penalty payments. Financial entities using designated critical providers must factor this oversight regime into their third-party risk management and ensure their contractual arrangements support the provider's compliance with oversight obligations.
