AI systems do not exist in isolation — they depend on data infrastructure that must itself be governed. Feature stores, analytics pipelines, data warehouses, and data lakes all feed into AI training and inference, and governance gaps in this infrastructure propagate directly into AI system quality and compliance risk. Our advisory engagement assesses the organization's data platform maturity specifically for AI workloads, identifying gaps in lineage tracking, access control, and version management that would undermine AI governance controls.

We establish feature store governance practices that ensure features used in AI models are versioned, documented, and reproducible. Analytics pipeline lineage tracking connects the dots from raw data sources through transformation layers to the features consumed by AI models, supporting provenance requirements and enabling root cause analysis when model behavior deviates. AI-specific access controls ensure that data access for training and inference follows the principle of least privilege and that sensitive data used in AI systems receives appropriate handling throughout the pipeline.
