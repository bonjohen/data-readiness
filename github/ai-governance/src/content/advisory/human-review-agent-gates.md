Agentic AI systems that take autonomous actions introduce oversight challenges that go beyond traditional model governance. When an AI agent can execute multi-step workflows, make API calls, or modify data without human intervention at each step, the oversight model must shift from reviewing every output to designing gates at critical decision points. Our advisory engagement helps organizations identify where gates should be placed, what criteria trigger escalation to a human reviewer, and how to avoid the twin failure modes of over-gating (which defeats the purpose of automation) and under-gating (which defeats the purpose of oversight).

We also address oversight fatigue — the documented tendency for human reviewers to rubber-stamp AI outputs when the approval rate is consistently high. Effective gate design includes attention-calibration mechanisms, rotation schedules, and adversarial testing of the review process itself to ensure that human oversight remains substantive rather than performative.
