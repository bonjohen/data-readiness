Prompt and response logs from AI systems frequently contain personal data — whether intentionally (when users input personal information) or incidentally (when AI responses reference individuals). Under the GDPR, these logs constitute personal data processing that requires a documented lawful basis under Art. 6, inclusion in the ROPA under Art. 30, and coverage by data subject rights including the right of access under Art. 15 and the right of erasure under Art. 17.

Organizations should establish clear retention policies for AI interaction logs that reflect the actual purpose of logging — whether for debugging, safety monitoring, service improvement, or regulatory compliance. Retaining logs indefinitely "for potential future use" violates the storage limitation principle under Art. 5(1)(e). Logs should be reviewed for personal data content, classified accordingly, and subject to the same access request and erasure workflows as other personal data stores. Where logs are used for model improvement, a separate lawful basis assessment is required, and data subjects should be informed through privacy notices.
