Article 22 gives data subjects the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing — including profiling — that produce legal effects or similarly significant effects. This provision directly applies to AI agents that make or influence decisions about individuals, such as credit scoring, automated hiring screens, insurance risk assessments, or content moderation affecting access to services. Organizations deploying AI agents must assess each decision point to determine whether Art. 22 is triggered and, if so, implement suitable safeguards including the right to obtain human intervention, express their point of view, and contest the decision.

The human review requirement under Art. 22(3) demands genuine human involvement, not a perfunctory sign-off on automated outputs. The human reviewer must have the authority and competence to override the automated decision and must actually exercise independent judgment. Organizations should document the review process, including the criteria reviewers apply, the frequency of overrides, and the training provided to reviewers. Where Art. 22(2) exceptions apply (contract necessity, legal authorization, or explicit consent), the organization must still implement suitable measures to safeguard the data subject's rights, freedoms, and legitimate interests.
