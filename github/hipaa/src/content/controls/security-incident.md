Security incident procedures under 45 CFR § 164.308(a)(6) require covered entities and business associates to implement policies and procedures to identify, respond to, and mitigate security incidents involving ePHI. All specifications in this domain are required — there are no addressable alternatives. Every organization handling ePHI must have a documented incident response capability.

The regulation defines a security incident as the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations. Organizations must be able to detect incidents through monitoring and logging, respond with defined procedures and roles, mitigate harmful effects, and document the entire lifecycle. The documentation requirement serves both compliance and continuous improvement purposes.

When a security incident constitutes a breach of unsecured ePHI, the Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR §§ 164.400-414) imposes additional requirements. Breaches affecting 500 or more individuals must be reported to HHS within 60 days and posted on the HHS Breach Portal. Affected individuals must be notified without unreasonable delay. Smaller breaches must be logged and reported to HHS annually. Organizations should conduct a four-factor risk assessment to determine whether an incident constitutes a reportable breach, considering the nature of the ePHI involved, the unauthorized person, whether ePHI was actually acquired or viewed, and the extent of mitigation.
