Every U.S. state with a comprehensive privacy law requires controllers to enter into contracts with processors that govern the processing of personal data. The required provisions are broadly consistent: Virginia (VCDPA s 59.1-578), Colorado (CPA s 6-1-1305), Connecticut (CTDPA s 42-521), Texas (TDPSA s 541.103), and Oregon (OCPA s 10) all mandate that processor agreements address the nature and purpose of processing, the type of data, the duration of processing, and the rights and obligations of both parties. However, the specific contractual requirements differ enough that a single-state DPA template will leave gaps when applied across jurisdictions.

| Provision | VA (VCDPA) | CO (CPA) | CT (CTDPA) | TX (TDPSA) | OR (OCPA) |
|-----------|------------|----------|------------|------------|-----------|
| Confidentiality | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Instruction limitation | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Audit rights | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Sub-processor notice | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| Data return/deletion | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |
| DPIA cooperation | Not specified | Required | Not specified | Not specified | Not specified |
| Demonstrate compliance | Required | Required | Required | Required | Required |

The practical approach is a harmonized DPA template that includes the union of all state requirements, with a jurisdiction annex that identifies which clauses are driven by which statutes. This avoids the need to maintain separate templates per state and ensures that every processor relationship is covered regardless of where consumers are located. The template should be reviewed against each new state law as it takes effect, because states entering the privacy landscape (e.g., Indiana ICDPA effective January 2026, Tennessee TIPA effective July 2025) may introduce unique provisions.

Organizations should maintain a processor register that tracks not only the identity and contact information of each processor but also the specific processing activities undertaken, the categories of personal data involved, and the state laws applicable to the relationship. This register serves as both an operational tool for managing DPA coverage and an evidence artifact for attorney general inquiries, which multiple state laws authorize.
