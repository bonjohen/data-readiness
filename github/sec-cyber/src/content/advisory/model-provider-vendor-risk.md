AI model providers — whether accessed via API, embedded in products, or used in internal tools — are third-party service providers whose cybersecurity posture directly affects the registrant. Item 106(b)(2) requires companies to describe their oversight of third-party risks, and model providers that process sensitive data, influence business decisions, or have access to proprietary information should be included in the vendor risk assessment program.

Concentration risk is a particular concern: heavy reliance on a single model provider creates a dependency that could be material if that provider suffers a cybersecurity incident. The vendor risk register should include model providers alongside traditional vendors, with assessment criteria tailored to AI-specific risks such as data leakage through model interfaces, prompt injection vulnerabilities, and training data contamination.
